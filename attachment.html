Attachment

What are attachment categories

Attachment is a term used by psychologists to describe the relationships between children and their parents and/or guardians. Developmental psychologists discovered four types of attachment categories: Secure, Avoidant, Ambivalent/Resistant, and Disorganized.
Research has found that attachment categories are not simply based on whether a caregiver feeds a child, but also whether they provide a safe haven and secure base for exploration for the child. These categories were constructed by developmental psychologist, Mary Ainsworth after having studied children and their parents in an observational experiment that she created known as the “Strange Situation”. (Ainsworth, Bell, & Stayton, 1971)
